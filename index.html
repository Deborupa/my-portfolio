Projects

Assessing Risk Factors Pertaining to Diabetes

• Background: The project investigated diabetes, a significant health issue, aiming to identify key predictors of diabetes status among patients using a comprehensive dataset.

• Need: Understanding which factors most influence diabetes status is crucial for early diagnosis, targeted interventions, and improved patient outcomes.

• Methodology: The study involved data preprocessing, exploratory data analysis, visualization, and statistical testing (including logistic regression and non-parametric tests) on data from 1,000 patients, focusing on variables like Age, HbA1c, Cholesterol, LDL, BMI, and Gender.

• Outcome: The analysis identified Age, HbA1c, Cholesterol, LDL, BMI, and Gender as significant predictors of diabetes status, providing valuable insights for personalized healthcare strategies and diabetes management.

Panic Disorder Prediction

• Background: Panic disorder significantly impacts quality of life, and data-driven approaches can enhance early detection and management.

• Need: Improved tools for early detection and prediction of panic disorder are needed to address gaps in current diagnostic methods.

• Methodology: Utilized Kaggle data, applied preprocessing techniques, and evaluated multiple machine learning models for predicting panic disorder.

• Outcome: Random Forest and Decision Tree models showed high accuracy in predicting panic disorder, indicating effective predictive capabilities.

Comprehensive Analysis of Drug Related Mortality Patterns

• Background: Detailed the ERD design, explaining tables such as Patients, Medications, AdverseEvents, Outcomes, and PatientMedications to manage relationships and track drug-related incidents.

• Need: Effective organization and tracking of patient-medication interactions and adverse events required a well-structured database to support accurate reporting and analysis.

• Methodology: Designed an ERD with tables for Patients, Medications, AdverseEvents, Outcomes, and PatientMedications. Implemented primary and foreign keys to define relationships and ensure data integrity, normalized to 3NF.

• Outcome: Achieved a well-organized database structure supporting complex queries and analyses, with clear referential integrity and the ability to track patient outcomes and medication-related adverse events.
